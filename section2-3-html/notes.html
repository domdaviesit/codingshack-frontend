Everything that follows a block element(uses all available space) gets pushed down the page.

div and span

They are generic and should be used when other elements aren't suitable.

div is block level and fills all the available width. Everything before the div is pushed up and after is pushed down.divs are great for grouping elements together and for areas that can be manipulated by JavaScript. Can put other elements in a div.

Spans are inline it's just a grouping element. Doesn't disrupt the flow of the html doc.
